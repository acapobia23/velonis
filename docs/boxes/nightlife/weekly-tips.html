Weekly Tips

The Weekend 04-05th October in Florence


Florence comes alive this first weekend of October with free museum entries,
vibrant markets, and unmissable exhibitions. Whether you're an art lover,
a vintage hunter, or simply looking to soak up the Florentine autumn atmosphere,
there's something special waiting around every corner.



Markets, Museums and Events


Sunday at the Museum

As on every first Sunday of the month, state museums are free in Florence!
From the Uffizi Galleries to the National Museum of the Bargello, all the way
to lesser-known gems—a perfect chance to discover something new without spending a euro.



Beato Angelico

The Fondazione Palazzo Strozzi and the Museum of San Marco present an extraordinary
exhibition dedicated to Fra Giovanni da Fiesole, a symbolic figure of 15th-century
Florentine art. A rare opportunity to immerse yourself in Renaissance spirituality and beauty.



Toulouse-Lautrec, a Journey into Belle Époque Paris

The Museo degli Innocenti hosts a new temporary exhibition exploring the life and work
of Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec, one of the most emblematic artists of the Belle Époque.
Step into the cabarets, cafés, and bohemian spirit of turn-of-the-century Paris.



The Rose That Grew From Concrete

This contemporary art group exhibition at the former Convent of Sant'Orsola features
fourteen artists who narrate the transformations of the complex through site-specific works.
They symbolically heal its wounds and pay tribute to the power of nature—
a poetic dialogue between art, architecture, and resilience.



Autumn Plant & Flower Fair

At the Giardino dell'Orticultura, nurseries from all over Tuscany showcase
an impressive floral exhibition with rare and unusual varieties in its autumn edition.
Perfect for plant lovers and anyone seeking a burst of natural color.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM



International Ceramics Fair

In Piazza Santa Croce, over 90 ceramic artists from across Europe exhibit their work—
from everyday objects to sculptures, from jewelry to design pieces—made with majolica,
stoneware, porcelain, raku, and many other techniques. A celebration of craftsmanship and creativity.

Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Florence Vintage Market

At Palazzo Corsini, the vintage market returns with high-quality donated clothing,
accessories, and objects—combining style, sustainability, and social commitment.
Shop with purpose and discover unique pieces with history.

Hours: 11:00 AM – 6:00 PM



Enjoy Market

In Piazza Indipendenza, explore a vibrant vintage and craft market featuring jewelry,
clothes, bags, accessories, home décor, gift ideas, and antiques. A treasure trove
for those who love one-of-a-kind finds.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Vintage Kilo Sale

At Manifattura Tabacchi, shop high-quality second-hand and vintage clothing sold by the kilo.
An eco-friendly way to refresh your wardrobe while embracing sustainable fashion.

Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Savonarola Antiquaria

On Sunday in Piazza Savonarola, browse through stands filled with rarities, antiques,
and collectibles: books, paintings, prints, porcelain, furniture, and other curiosities
from the past. A must for antique enthusiasts and curious souls.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Car Bazar

On Sunday at the Ippodromo del Visarno, explore a unique market where vendors sell
directly from their car trunks. A mini-market of vintage, creativity, second-hand,
and garage sale treasures. You never know what gem you might find!

Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Only Usato Market

On Sunday at the Orti Dipinti in Borgo Pinti, discover a charming second-hand market
for clothing, accessories, and small home items—all surrounded by plants, flowers,
vegetables, and even chickens. A quirky, green oasis in the heart of the city.

Hours: 11:00 AM – 7:00 PM
