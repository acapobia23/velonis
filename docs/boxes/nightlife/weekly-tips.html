Weekly Tips

The Weekend - July 26-27 in Florence

THE WEEKEND 13-14th SEPTEMBER IN FLORENCE



EVENTS / MUSEUMS

Fashion in the Spotlight 1925-1955. The Origins of Made in Italy: The Museum of Fashion and Costume at Palazzo Pitti is hosting an exhibition featuring over fifty garments, accessories, and a mix of videos and photographs that trace thirty key years in the history of Italian fashion - from 1925 to 1955.


Museo Diffuso. Want to see Florence from above and explore hidden views of the city? Discover Florence’s history and how the city grew over time by visiting its towers, gates, and fortresses - including Porta San Frediano, Torre della Zecca, Torre San Niccolò, and Fortezza da Basso. More info at www.musefirenze.it.

Corri la Vita. A Sunday of culture and sport for a good cause! On one side, there’s a charity run to raise funds for breast cancer care and prevention. On the other, you’ll get free access to over 30 cultural sites across Florence and much of Tuscany (details at www.corrilavita.it), including a special tour through the Medici villas of Castello, Petraia, Poggio a Caiano, and Cerreto Guidi.

MARKETS AND FOOD

French Regional Market. Back in Florence in Piazza Santa Croce from September 11 to 14! The sights, smells, and flavors of France fill the square with high-quality, traditional French food and specialties (H 9-21).


Ognissanti in Festa. This Saturday in Piazza Ognissanti, check out a market fair dedicated to vintage collectibles and creative handmade crafts. A mix of nostalgia, one-of-a-kind finds, and artistic flair (H 10-20).

Artigianato e Palazzo. From the 12th to the 14th, Giardino Corsini celebrates fine craftsmanship in a modern way — showcasing everything from decor, ceramics, jewelry, and textiles, to woodworking, leather, marble, and gourmet food.

Firenze Jazz Festival. Final weekend to catch the Festival! Big names from the Italian and international jazz scene perform in amazing venues like Forte Belvedere, Villa Bardini, the Villa Strozzi Amphitheater, and the Medici Park of Pratolino. Info: firenzejazzfestival.it.

Pitti Fragranze. Until Sunday, Stazione Leopolda hosts the international salon dedicated to artistic perfumery and cutting-edge skincare (H 10-18).

Only Usato Market. Spend your Saturday afternoon shopping second-hand and vintage clothing, home goods, and more at Atollo, a cool spot on Lungarno Ferrucci (H 15-23).

Creative Factory. All weekend in Piazza dei Ciompi, meet local artisans, makers, and designers showing off their handmade creations (H 10-20).

Botanica Urbana. Head to Manifattura Tabacchi this weekend for a plant and flower market with a curated selection of sellers, talks, workshops, and family-friendly activities (H 9-19).

Fiera d’Oltrarno. On Sunday in Piazza Santo Spirito, browse a lively market with everything from handmade crafts to antiques and local products (H 9-19).

Fierucolina. In Piazza del Carmine on Sunday morning, check out this small-scale farmers’ market with organic produce, handmade crafts, and rustic vibes (H 9-14).

Expo Chianti Classico. Greve in Chianti hosts over 100 Chianti Classico wine producers — it’s the perfect excuse to explore the land of the famous Vino Chianti Classico!

Mare Mood Festival. From Thursday the 11th to Saturday the 13th, Limonaia Strozzi becomes seafood heaven — taste fresh summer dishes and enjoy a delicious goodbye to the season! (H 19-23:30).
