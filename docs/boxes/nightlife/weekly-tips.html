Weekly Tips

The Weekend 18-19th October in Florence


This weekend Florence celebrates art, craftsmanship, and the rich flavors
of autumn. From Renaissance masterpieces to Belle Époque Paris, from artisan
markets to wine tastings, October offers the perfect blend of culture, creativity,
and seasonal delights. Get ready to explore, taste, and discover!



Markets, Museums and Events


Beato Angelico

Palazzo Strozzi Foundation and the Museum of San Marco present an extraordinary exhibition
dedicated to Fra Giovanni da Fiesole, a key figure of 15th-century Florentine art.
A rare opportunity to immerse yourself in Renaissance spirituality and beauty.



Toulouse-Lautrec: A Journey into Belle Époque Paris

The Museum of the Innocents hosts a new temporary exhibit focused on one of the most
iconic artists of the Belle Époque, Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec. Step into the cabarets,
cafés, and bohemian spirit of turn-of-the-century Paris.



The Rose That Grew From Concrete

A contemporary art group show at the former Convent of Sant'Orsola. Through site-specific
works, 14 artists symbolically heal the space's wounds and celebrate nature's resilience.
A poetic dialogue between art, architecture, and transformation.



Uniqo Market

Three days in Piazza Santa Croce to explore unique creations, original pieces, and the
talent of carefully selected artisans. Perfect for finding one-of-a-kind handmade treasures.

Hours: 10:00 AM – 8:00 PM



Collecting in Florence

This weekend, the underground lot at the Cartiere Carrara Theater turns into a market
of collectibles, vintage items, and small antiques. A treasure trove for collectors and
enthusiasts of the past.

Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM



Indipendenza Antiquaria

Piazza Indipendenza fills up with 110 stands offering furniture, books, prints, sculptures,
paintings, and much more. A beloved market celebrating antiques and vintage finds.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM



In Villa with the Artisans

Italian craftsmanship at its finest returns to Villa Il Padule in Bagno a Ripoli for
a high-end artisan market. Support local talent and discover exceptional handmade pieces.

Hours: 10:30 AM – 7:30 PM



Rionalissima

The big fall market of local vendors takes over the streets around the Artemio Franchi
Stadium this Sunday. A neighborhood tradition celebrating community and local commerce.



Only Usato

Sunday at the Giardino dell'Orticultura is all about secondhand: clothes, accessories,
vinyl, books, plus workshops, food, music, and more. A sustainable shopping experience
in a beautiful garden setting.

Hours: 11:00 AM – 6:00 PM



Fierucola di Santo Spirito

Sunday in Piazza Santo Spirito, discover small-scale organic produce, handmade crafts,
and traditional local goods. Taste the authentic flavors of Tuscan countryside brought
directly to the heart of Florence.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM



DegustaVino

Sunday, Via Gioberti turns into an open-air wine cellar where you can taste and buy
directly from Chianti Rufina winemakers. Over 50 DOC and DOCG wines to try! A true
celebration of Tuscan wine tradition.

Hours: 12:00 PM – 9:00 PM



Pumpkin Kingdom

At Villa Montalvo Park in Campi Bisenzio, you'll find a full-on pumpkin village—
an immersive experience celebrating this autumn favorite. Perfect for families and
anyone who loves the magic of the fall season.



Chestnut Festival

Fall in Tuscany means celebrating one of its seasonal stars: chestnuts! Head to San Piero
a Sieve, Marradi, or anywhere in the Mugello for chestnut treats in all kinds of delicious
forms. From roasted to desserts, a true taste of autumn.
