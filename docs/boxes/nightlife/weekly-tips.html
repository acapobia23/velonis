THE WEEKEND 06-07th SEPTEMBER IN FLORENCE



Festa della Rificolona



Since the mid-1600s, on the evening of September 7th, farmers and mountain folks would walk into Florence with their wives to honor the birth of the Virgin Mary, celebrated the next day in Piazza Santissima Annunziata.

The journey took all night, and to light the way, they carried handmade paper lanterns lit with candles and hung from small sticks. That’s how the Rificolona parade was born — a colorful tradition still alive today!

The procession is about 11 kilometers long, starting at 4:00 PM from the Basilica of Impruneta and ending around 9:30 PM in Piazza Santissima Annunziata, where the square lights up with hundreds of bright, colorful lanterns in a truly magical atmosphere.



Markets, Food and Events



Free Museum Sunday: Like every first Sunday of the month, state museums are free in Florence too! From the Uffizi Galleries to the Bargello National Museum — and even lesser-known spots worth discovering.


Open-Air Museum: Want to admire Florence from above or explore the city’s urban history? Climb towers, gates, and fortresses like Porta San Frediano, Torre della Zecca, Torre San Niccolò, and Fortezza da Basso. More info: www.musefirenze.it


Firenze Comics: Head to Villa Montalvo park in Campi Bisenzio this weekend for cosplay contests, meetups, parades, artists, writers, comic book creators, gaming zones, Japanese food, live music, and much more! (10-19)


Fair Priced Vintage: Europe’s biggest vintage market stops in Florence at Teatro Cartiere Carrara – perfect for giving clothes and accessories a second life!


Ars Manualis: Traditional skills and crafts take center stage in Piazza Santissima Annunziata with a high-quality artisan market. (Friday & Saturday, 10-20)


Savonarola Antiquaria: On Sunday, browse the antique fair in Piazza Savonarola — great for treasure hunters and vintage lovers


Fierucola del Pane: Also on Sunday in Piazza SS. Annunziata — the beloved bread fair, with handmade and local food products. (9-19)


Firenze Jazz Festival: Music is everywhere! Over 100 artists and tons of concerts across the city until September 14. Full schedule at: www.firenzejazzfestival.it


Copula Mundi: al Lumen – Parco a Mensola – A free-entry experimental festival with performances, workshops, talks, street art, activities for kids, sports, theater shows, concerts, and DJ sets


Yoga Festival: Parco di Villa Vogel will host this free event open to all ages, dedicated to yoga, meditation, music, and a variety of holistic practices
