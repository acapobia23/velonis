Weekly Tips

THE WEEKEND 05-06th JULY IN FLORENCE

Events, Museums, Markets and Food:

- Free Museum Sunday: Like every first Sunday of the month, Florence’s city museums are free to visit — from the Uffizi Galleries to the Bargello National Museum, and even lesser-known spots just waiting to be discovered

- Open-Air Museum: Want a fresh view of Florence? Towers, gates, and city fortresses are open to the public — like Porta San Frediano, Torre della Zecca, Torre San Niccolò, and Fortezza da Basso. It’s a great way to explore the city’s history and layout. (More info: www.musefirenze.it)

- Forte Belvedere: Built in the late 1500s as a fortified citadel to protect the Oltrarno area, it’s now open with a new exhibition about Florence’s story and identity, part of the "Firenze Forma Continua" project. Open 10 AM – 8 PM.

- Outdoor Cinema:
Apriti Cinema: Free open-air screenings in the courtyard of Palazzo Pitti, every evening at 9:45 PM (program)
Cinema in Manifattura: The “In Cantiere” series offers premieres, classics, films in original languages (with Italian subtitles), kids’ movies, and documentaries every evening at Manifattura Tabacchi (tickets & info)
Cinema nel Chiostro: Films in original language shown every night in the courtyard of the former Sant’Orsola convent, in collaboration with Spazio Alfieri (tickets & info)
Cinema in Villa: One of Italy’s most scenic outdoor cinemas, held on the stunning terrace of Villa Bardini overlooking Florence (info)

- Archaeology Nights: Visit the Fiesole Archaeological Area at sunset or even sunrise, with special events ranging from ancient theater to mythology (info)

- Isabella’s Night: This weekend in the historic center of Cerreto Guidi, dive into a Renaissance-style historical reenactment — think sword fighting, falconry, flag performances, music, dancing, fire shows, magicians, and even puppet theater for kids

- European Beer Festival: In Reggello from 7 PM over the weekend, enjoy a wide variety of beers. Alongside Italian craft brews, there’ll be international guests from Germany, Austria, Poland, and France serving up their beer and food specialties.

- From Wheat to Plate: On Saturday starting at 10 AM in Strada in Chianti, celebrate rural traditions and handmade fresh pasta with this delicious food-themed event

- Sagre: This weekend, the areas around Florence are full of tasty local food festivals!
Saturday is your last chance to check out the Tortello Festival in Pelago (Sagra del Tortello alla Pelaghese).
Sunday, head to Palazzuolo sul Senio for another Tortello Festival.
On both Saturday and Sunday, you’ve got plenty more options:
At the Tamburello sports field in Bassa (Cerreto Guidi), there’s a Fish Festival.
In Spicchio (Vinci), it’s all about the Snail Festival.
In Sammontana (Montelupo Fiorentino), enjoy the Country-Style Food Festival (Sagra Campagnola).
And in Pozzolatico (Impruneta), don’t miss the Steak and Porcini Mushroom Festival.
