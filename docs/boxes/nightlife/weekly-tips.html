Weekly Tips

The Weekend 1st-2nd November in Florence


This weekend Florence opens its museums for free, celebrates underwater photography,
and fills its squares with artisan markets and autumn festivities.
From Renaissance masterpieces to contemporary art, from handcrafted goods to vintage treasures,
the first weekend of November offers a perfect blend of culture, shopping, and seasonal fun.
Get ready to explore, discover, and enjoy!



Sunday at the Museum

Like every first Sunday of the month, state museums are free also in Florence.
From the Uffizi Galleries to the Bargello National Museum to lesser-known places,
but all to be discovered. A perfect opportunity to immerse yourself in art and history!



Oceans. David Doubilet at Villa Bardini

Dive into the boundless beauty of life in the ocean depths and the fragility of underwater ecosystems.
For the first time, National Geographic comes to Florence to celebrate the work of David Doubilet,
the legendary underwater photographer and pioneer of ocean exploration.



Beato Angelico at Palazzo Strozzi

Fondazione Palazzo Strozzi and the Museo di San Marco present Beato Angelico,
an extraordinary exhibition dedicated to Fra Giovanni da Fiesole,
a true symbol of 15th-century Florentine art.



Toulouse-Lautrec at Museo degli Innocenti

The Museo degli Innocenti hosts a new temporary exhibition exploring the life and work
of one of the most iconic artists of the Belle Époque: Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec.
A journey into the Paris of the Belle Époque.



The Rose That Grew From Concrete

This group exhibition of contemporary art takes place in the former Convent of Sant'Orsola.
Through site-specific works, fourteen artists tell the story of the site's transformations,
symbolically healing its wounds and paying tribute to the strength of nature.



Ars Manualis: The Legacy of Craftsmanship

Piazza Santa Maria Novella turns into an open-air art market showcasing top-notch
handmade crafts and local artisans.

When: 10 AM - 8 PM



All Saints Fair - Sunday 2nd November

Sunday at Parco delle Cascine, the big autumn market is back with over 300 stalls
selling all kinds of stuff. A perfect opportunity for bargain hunters and market lovers!

When: 8 AM - 8 PM



Textile Arts Fair

This weekend in Piazza Santa Croce, check out the market-exhibition dedicated to local wool and yarns,
with shopping, workshops, and live demos. A celebration of textile craftsmanship!

When: 9:30 AM - 7 PM



Savonarola Antiquarian Market - Sunday 2nd November

Sunday in Piazza Savonarola, browse around looking for rare finds,
antiques, and vintage treasures. Perfect for collectors and antique lovers!

When: 8 AM - 8 PM



Car Bazar - Sunday 2nd November

Sunday at the Visarno Racetrack, the quirky flea market where sellers use their car trunks as stalls is back!
Expect vintage, handmade goods, second-hand items, and "everything must go" vibes.

When: 10 AM - 5 PM



The Pumpkin Kingdom

This weekend at Villa Montalvo Park in Campi Bisenzio, dive into autumn magic
with over 20,000 pumpkin sculptures, fun games, and plenty of street food.
A perfect family outing to celebrate the season!

When: 9 AM - 9 PM
